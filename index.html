Marketecture

Role: AI & Marketing Intern


Overview

As an AI & Marketing Intern at a social media marketing firm, I led the development of an internal AI-powered platform to automate content generation for clients. The tool was designed to deeply understand a brand‚Äôs voice and positioning through onboarding inputs and documents, and generate highly personalized content and strategy recommendations.


Problem

The firm‚Äôs clients needed consistent, high-quality marketing content that aligned with their brand voice‚Äîbut internal consultants were spending significant time writing briefs and generating ideas manually. We saw an opportunity to streamline this with a custom AI tool that understood each brand's unique identity and tone.


User Research Insights

Through conversations with the firm, I identified key pain points:

  • Generic AI tools like ChatGPT lacked context and produced off-brand outputs
  • ChatGPT pro was adept at generating content but it meant the firm staff had to create GPT's for each brand and their clients weren't always tech savy enough to figure out how to best make use of their personal GPT.
  • Firm staff wasted time rewriting or re-explaining brand tone
  • Clients often needed inspiration and competitor context alongside text suggestions


Product Approach

I built the tool using Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG), integrating OpenAI's API with a custom knowledge base sourced from past client work, internal brand strategy docs, and onboarding transcripts. I also designed a brand onboarding flow that generated a persistent "brand profile"‚Äîa rich context layer used to ground all content generation consisting of an inspiration bank and customer avatar.


Key Features

  • AI chatbot with retrieval-augmented content generation grounded in firm-specific strategy docs
  • Brand onboarding flow that creates a profile with customer avatar, tone guidelines, and example voice
  • Competitor analyzer that surfaces stylistic differences between brands using scraped content
  • Inspiration bank and copy templates tailored to each brand‚Äôs vibe and goals

Beeline TagTeam Reader

Coursework: CS51/52 (Project-based social impact x software engineering class)


Overview

Built in collaboration with peers for Beeline, an edtech startup. We designed and prototyped an AI-powered reading platform for early learners. I contributed to product direction, UX design decisions, and full-stack development using MERN and Anthropic's Claude API.


Problem

Many students‚Äîespecially in under-resourced schools‚Äîlack access to leveled, engaging reading material. Teachers struggle to find content that matches both students‚Äô interests and reading levels. Beeline wanted to explore a collaborative and AI-enhanced solution to support early literacy.


User Research Insights

We interviewed teachers and reviewed EdTech tools in use today. Key findings:

  • Students stay more engaged when reading material is tailored to their interests
  • Teachers need quick ways to monitor comprehension without heavy grading load
  • Collaborative or gamified formats increase participation


Product Approach

We focused on building a lightweight, modular prototype to test the core hypothesis: Can AI-generated readings + collaborative sessions improve student engagement and comprehension?


Key Features

  • AI-generated short readings based on chosen topics and reading level
  • Quiz generation tied directly to the reading content
  • Kahoot-style collaborative sessions for peer learning
  • Automatic results sharing with teachers to support formative assessment

Cape Colony Land Grants Map

Role: Research Assistant (Digital History Project)


Overview

I supported the digital restoration of ‚ÄúThe Southwestern Cape Colony, 1657‚Äì1750: Freehold Land Grants,‚Äù a historical text documenting early Dutch settlements in South Africa. The goal was to make the information spatially explorable and accessible to researchers, educators, and the public.


Problem

The original document existed only as a scanned printout, making it difficult to analyze spatial patterns or connect land grants with historical timelines. Our challenge was to transform it into a georeferenced, interactive dataset and map-based interface.


Process

  • Uploaded and aligned scanned maps using QGIS
  • Georeferenced and digitized each individual land grant with coordinates and metadata
  • Exported the dataset into a usable format for frontend development
  • Built a web interface allowing users to search by farm name, owner, and year
